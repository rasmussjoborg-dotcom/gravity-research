Spending Psychology
Refined

This component explains the psychological biases that drive habitual overspending and how Shift's AI-powered nudges are designed to counteract them.

  • People consistently underestimate the long-term financial impact of small, recurring purchases, leading to systematic overspending.
  • There's a significant gap between knowing one spends 'too much' and actually changing behavior, often due to impulsive (System 1) thinking overriding rational planning.
  • Shift's AI targets core cognitive biases like Present Bias (favoring immediate rewards) and Mental Accounting (categorizing money differently).
  • Shift's design aims to create temporal distance from spending impulses, fostering reflection and awareness *after* the fact, rather than relying on willpower in the moment.
