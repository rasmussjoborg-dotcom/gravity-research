Competitive Landscape
Drafted

This component analyzes the Swedish competitive landscape, revealing a significant, underserved opportunity for Shieldy's AI-driven D2C policy overlap detection due to a lack of explicit features from incumbents.

  • The Swedish market has a confirmed, underserved gap for D2C AI-driven overlap detection and savings in existing insurance policies.
  • This gap represents a substantial 'lazy tax' opportunity of €200-500M annually from undetected overlaps in multi-policy households.
  • Key players like aggregators (Compricer, Zmarta) and major banks (Swedbank, SEB) do not offer explicit overlap detection, focusing instead on new policy comparisons.
  • Insurely provides critical market data access (95% coverage) but relies on partners to build the consumer-facing tools for overlap alerts and savings, positioning them as an enabler rather than a direct D2C competitor.
90% researched
Still needs:Specific user acquisition costs (CAC) or conversion rates for insurance switching among aggregator platforms (Compricer, Zmarta).

Coverage Fragmentation
Drafted

This component identifies where users are paying for the same insurance coverage multiple times, highlighting potential savings from redundant policies.

  • Many users pay a 'lazy tax' due to fragmented insurance portfolios, holding multiple policies that cover the same risks.
  • The most common and quantifiable overlaps are between premium credit card travel insurance and dedicated travel insurance policies.
  • Swedish home insurance ('hemförsäkring') with 'drulle' (all-risk) coverage also presents potential overlaps with travel and device insurance.
  • Overlaps lead to redundant premiums, annual fees, and benefits that are often secondary or can only be claimed once, wasting user money.
75% researched
Still needs:Validate the 15-20% savings figure specifically for the Swedish market.Quantify the exact scope of travel and device coverage within typical Swedish home insurance policies and how it overlaps with other benefits.Further research on overlaps between health insurance and gym/accident coverage.

Swedish Insurance Mechanics
Drafted

The Swedish insurance market presents a significant 'lazy tax' opportunity for Shieldy due to widespread collective policy overlaps, but the MVP must rely on manual/hybrid data input as programmatic access is currently infeasible.

  • Manual/hybrid data input (PDF upload, OCR, user input) is mandatory for the MVP due to regulatory and technical barriers.
  • The 'lazy tax' is strongly validated, with 20-30% of middle-class Swedes holding redundant policies, costing SEK 5,000-15,000 annually per household.
  • Shieldy's initial target audience should be the Swedish middle class (70-80% covered by collective agreements) who are unaware of their policy overlaps.
  • There's a substantial market opportunity of SEK 10-20 billion in unoptimized premiums.
90% researched
Still needs:Further research on specific GDPR risks associated with BankID-authenticated OCR and potential mitigation strategies.Clarification on the implications of 'Limited Voluntary Health' insurance in the Swedish market.
